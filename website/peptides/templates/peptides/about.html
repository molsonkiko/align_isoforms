About the website

Back to proteins list

The purpose of this site is to educate biologists about the possibility of using mass spectrometric data to differentiate between splicing variants (hereafter called isoforms) of proteins that have some harmless isoforms and others that are potentially oncogenic.

The site was constructed on behalf of biology professor Les Timpe, who has been studying this topic for some time.

Gillette et al. (2020, Cell 182, 200-225 July 9, 2020) created a database of peptides found in the mass spectra of different protein isoforms that differentiate between their splicing variants.

The red regions of the above proteins are mass spec peptides found by Gillette et al. that can be used to differentiate between the isoforms.

This method is far from foolproof. The stochastic nature of protein fragmentation during mass spectrometry means that many proteins do not have peptides that are useful for this diagnostic purpose. However, by examining proteins with peptides in the database and using the visualizations tools on this website, hopefully it will become apparent how mass spectrometric data can be used as a diagnostic tool to determine if an oncogenic variant of a protein is present in a tissue sample.
